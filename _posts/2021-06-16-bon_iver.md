I remember where I was the first time I heard Bon Iver. I remember how I felt when I first listened; like I had been enevolped in a warm blanket. It was perfect, just what I needed without me knowing it. I have remained close to that first album ever since and even used it as a way to get over a sudden and inexplicable fear of flying I developed a few years ago. I would get a good book and read it as I queued to get on the plane. I would put on For Emma, Forever Ago and just zone out until we were in the sky. The familiarity of the lyrics didn't distract from my reading the book and the atmospheric orchestra of sound returned me to that cocoon of safety I felt all those year ago when I heard it for the first time.

Justin Vernon famously went to live in his father's cabin in Wisconsin to record the album and it's both the perfect backstory and the ideal narrative for the album. He emerged with an album as the snow thawed after a transformative experience. A transformative experience he catalogued in his songs. And then he shared them with us and transformed us too.

**How to create a mood**

Bon Iver's ability to create a mood is what makes me return to him time and time again. Maybe that's what all music does. It takes us somewhere. Bon Iver manages to supplant us in the middle of a nowhere. As I embarked on this data exploratory analysis, I picked Bon Iver because he intrigues me. I feel like I understand his music without understanding what exactly it is that his music does to me. There's something very subtle about his music.
