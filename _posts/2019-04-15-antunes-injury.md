## Victor Antunes’ leg buckled underneath him on Sunday afternoon during the game against Real Valladolid and the reports that followed were not good. He could be out for the season as Getafe chase LaLiga's fourth and final spot in the Champions League.

He arrived from Dynamo Kiev last summer after a successful loan spell the year before which saw Getafe finish 7th with the very best LaLiga has to offer ahead of them. Antunes himself is having an excellent season and Getafe as a club are exceeding all expectations too. They are behind Sevilla, for now, in fifth place but they have a chance to make it into that final Champions League spot even if Antunes injury comes at a terrible times for them.

Antunes, despite being 32, is not only one of the most consistent performances for Bordalas’ side but he is one of the most productive too. His delivery from the left is always one of the most dangerous aspects of their play and while Getafe have become known for their no-nonsense defending, Antunes is more like an attacking wing-back than a true full-back.
